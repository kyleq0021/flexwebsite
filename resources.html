RESOURCES

The American Psychological Association has advice on how parents, teachers and kids can take ation to prvent bullying. Also suggestions on how to respond and a section on cyberbullying.

The It Gets Better Project has inspiring advice for lesbian, gay, bisexual and transgender (LGBT) youth, helping them understand that “it gets better” and to inspire the changes needed to make it better.

The Crisis Support Services of Navada has free confidential 24-hour hotlines that anyone can call or text regarding bullying, assault, and depression. People can talk to someone, recieve support, and be provided with further resources.

On StopBullying.gov there is a well curated list of resources from the government on bullying and cyberbullying. It not only defines bullying but has resources on how to repond to it and how to “be more than a bystander.”
